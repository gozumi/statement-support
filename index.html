  • We Will Be Martians

    The brief for We Will Be Martians was to create an original soundtrack for the provided film ‘We Will be Martians’, which is part of the Music Tech A Level exam. This was my first time attempting to create a 3 minute soundtrack for a space-related film.

    Upon initially watching the film, my first thoughts were that I wanted to create a story through my sound. As the film could be interpreted as a series of ‘outer space’ scenes with no correlation, my aim was to link the images with music and create a narrative. Furthermore, I set out to match most of the moving images with sounds that would be associated with the image e.g. the loud rumbling of a rocket ship. However, I didn’t want to give sound to each and every movement in the film, as that would make the piece become predictable. This decision was also made so as to leave the viewer with the freedom to imagine what sounds could have been used in the ‘empty moments’, giving the piece more interest and room for thought and discussion by the audience.

    I put together my story through my sound by creating motifs for specific scenes and images that reoccur in the film, for example, the use of the triangle when the flower seeds enter the frame. The use of the 4 note pattern was a strong highlight of this, as it reappears towards the end of the extract but is played through the strings instead of the synths heard at the start.

    As this piece is still a work in progress, I would like to adjust the levels of all the tracks further to reduce the distortion of the sound. Additionally, to fit the A Level brief, I will adjust some of the Logic stock synths and change the tracks to original synths I have created, and use some samples in the piece too - both original and others already created.

  • Rain Sinattra

    Rain Sinattra is a jazz extract that I was asked to produce for a scene within a short film. The brief was to create an up-tempo jazz track matching 140 bpm.

  • Jungle Space

    Jungle Space was an early house piece I created using Logic stock loops. This was a piece I made to challenge myself in terms of constructing an afro-house styled track that continuously engaged the listener, whilst using your traditional repeated house music format.

  • D Minor Concerto (unfinished)

    D Minor Concerto: This is an unfinished Baroque Concerto in D minor that I created as a music A Level composition. I used the Logic stocks instruments however, to approve the work my intention is to use the plug-in ‘Omnisphere’ as VST’s The aim to complete this piece of music is to add a final episode towards the end of the extract and then conclude the piece with the last ritornello in the Tonic Key.

  • VVS Neck

    VVS neck is a melodic drill track made for an artist to rap (freestyle) over. It uses the conventional drill 808 glides as well as the fast bpm to match the urban sounding genre.

  • Matrix

    Matrix is an experimental fusion excerpt I constructed to match the scene of an character walking through tough terrain for countless years - this led me to call it matrix as a metaphor to resemble this character's journey. My inspiration was taken from an artistic short film I experienced by Kehinde Wiley called ‘in search of the miraculous’.
